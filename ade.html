Nelson Mandela

1918-2013

A south Afrcan anti-aparthied revolutionary

He was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary political leader and philanthropist who served as President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election. His government focused on dismantling the legacy of apartheid by tackling institutionalised racism and fostering racial reconciliation. Ideologically an African nationalist and socialist, he served as the president of the African National Congress (ANC) party from 1991 to 1997.
Mandela

The following is a time line of Mandela's life

  • 1918 - born in the village of Mvezo in Umtata South Africa
  • 1933 - he began his secondary education at Clarkebury Methodist High School in Engcobo
  • 1939 - he began work on a BA degree at the University of Fort Hare
  • 1943–1949 - Law studies and the ANC Youth League
  • 1950–1954 - Defiance Campaign and Transvaal ANC Presidency
  • 1955–1961 - Congress of the People and the Treason Trial
  • 1961–1962 - MK, the SACP, and African tour
  • 1962–1964 - Arrest and Rivonia trial
  • 1964–1982 - Robben Island
  • 1982–1988 - Pollsmoor Prison
  • 1988–1990 - Victor Verster Prison and release
  • 1990–1991 - End of apartheid early negotiations
  • 1994–1999 - Presidency of South Africa
  • 1999–2004 - Continued activism and philanthropy
  • 2004–2013 - Retiring from retirement
  • 2011–2013 - Illness and death. In February 2011, Mandela was briefly hospitalised with a respiratory infection, attracting international attention, before being re-admitted for a lung infection and gallstone removal in December 2012. After a successful medical procedure in early March 2013, his lung infection recurred and he was briefly hospitalised in Pretoria. In June 2013, his lung infection worsened and he was readmitted to a Pretoria hospital in serious condition. After suffering from a prolonged respiratory infection, Mandela died on 5 December 2013 at the age of 95, at around 20:50 local time (UTC+2) at his home in Houghton, surrounded by his family.
